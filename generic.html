Profile

I am Bartholomeow Ayinbila Aobe, a seasoned Project Manager specializing in Monitoring, Evaluation & Learning (MEL) at GOPA Worldwide Consultants. With over 9 years of experience, I have worked across Sub-Saharan Africa and Southeast Asia, driving impactful projects in international development cooperation. I hold a Master’s in Public Economics, Law & Politics from Leuphana University, Germany, and a Bachelor’s in Economics from the University of Ghana. Additionally, I completed a postgraduate study in International Development Cooperation at Humboldt University, Berlin.

Expertise & Skills

My expertise spans project monitoring & evaluation, impact assessments, and policy analysis, working with global organizations such as the EU, GIZ, UN, and SDC. I am proficient in STATA, Python, R, and online survey tools such as KoboCollect, Lime Survey, SurveyCTO and Survey Solutions for data analysis. I have extensive experience in M&E frameworks, including logical frameworks, Theory of Change, and OECD-DAC evaluation criteria. My work involves designing and implementing robust M&E systems to track development projects’ efficiency and effectiveness.

Projects Managed

I have managed and supported key international development projects, including the EU-EAC CORE Programme, Agribusiness Facility for Africa (ABF), and impact assessments in Bangladesh, Cameroon, and Rwanda. My research contributions include a meta-evaluation study on scientific rigor and a published evaluation on transboundary conservation in the SADC region. I have also participated in youth leadership initiatives and global policy dialogues, such as the Afrika Dialog from the German Foreign Ministry and Perspektivewerk under the Mercator Foundation in Germany.

Language Skills and Networks

I'm Fluent in English (C2) and German (C1) and frequently engage with a wide range of stakeholders across Africa, Europe, and Asia. I am an active DAAD Alumnus and a member of the Leuphana and SLE Alumni networks.
